# Close-reading protocol

Analyze only what the paper supports:

1. Bibliographic identity and research area.
2. Research question and why it matters.
3. Model, assumptions, notation, and data-generating process.
4. Estimator, algorithm, theorem, proof strategy, or identification argument.
5. Main theoretical and empirical results, with conditions.
6. Baselines and evaluation design.
7. Claimed contribution versus the reader's interpretation.
8. Limitations, fragile assumptions, and unresolved questions.
9. Connections and disagreements with other indexed papers.
10. Reusable ideas for the user's manuscript.

Attach page evidence to important factual statements. Mark uncertain interpretations explicitly.
